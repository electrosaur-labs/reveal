🎨 Posterization Settings
Reduce your image to a limited color palette for screen printing. Choose your engine, color mode, and target palette size.
Engine & Strategy
Reveal ensures all hues are represented (best for complex images). Balanced is faster with good quality. Classic is fastest for quick tests. Stencil creates grayscale separations (L-channel only).
SALIENCY: Averages the top 5% most vibrant/bright pixels to capture the "soul" of each color. Prevents muddy washout. Best for portraits with skin tones (e.g., JethroAsMonroe.tif). VOLUMETRIC: Standard weighted average respecting pixel frequency. Smooth and stable. Best for photos with soft gradients.

Substrate Handling
Excludes substrate color from palette, saving slots for artwork colors. Auto-Detect samples image corners to identify substrate. None for full-bleed designs with no background.
Substrate Tolerance (ΔE) 3.5
1.0 8.0
How aggressively to filter background pixels. Lower = more selective (only exact matches). Higher = more aggressive (includes similar shades). Typical: 3.0-4.0 for clean backgrounds, 5.0-6.0 for textured substrates.

Merge similar colors to optimize for screen printing. Only runs when palette exceeds target count.

Minimum hue difference (degrees) to prevent distinct color families from merging during pruning. Protects: Green from Beige, Yellow skin from Neutral backgrounds. JethroAsMonroe tip: Use 12-15° for tight hue family isolation.

L-value ceiling for shadow protection. Prevents deep shadows (L<15) from merging with lighter colors. Critical for halftones: Keeps neutral black dots from merging into skin tones or backgrounds. Lower: protect only deepest shadows, Higher: protect more dark colors.

Dithering
None: Hard color boundaries (posterized look). Fast. Best for graphic art.
Floyd-Steinberg: Error diffusion creates smooth gradients. Best for photographic images.
Blue Noise: Dispersed dot patterns. Best for screen printing (prevents worming artifacts).
Bayer 8x8: Regular crosshatch pattern. Retro appearance with predictable structure.
Atkinson: High-contrast dithering (75% error distribution). Crisp output with strong edges.
Stucki: High-fidelity error diffusion. Best for smooth photographic transitions and skin tones.
Rule of 7: Max halftone LPI = Mesh TPI / 7.
Pixel-Level: Finest detail, no mesh constraint (default).
Mesh-Aware: Clustered dots ensure minimum printable size on your screen mesh.
Higher mesh = finer detail but harder to print. Lower mesh = easier to print, coarser halftones.
Edge Quality
Gray Gamma 2.2 provides hard edges ideal for screen printing's binary ink coverage. Dot Gain 20% softens edges for offset printing workflows.
